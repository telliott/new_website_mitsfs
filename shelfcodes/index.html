Shelfcodes

A shelfcode is used to describe the type of book in the library, and which section it can be found in. They consist of three optional parts, always in the same order. The three parts are:

[circulation status/][size-][type]

The separation characters are omitted if that section is omitted

Circulation status:
SR Special Reserve. These books cannot be taken out of the library and can only be read with a keyholder present or with Skinnerial permission.
R Reserve. These books do not circulate outside the library and cannot be checked out
(none) Books without a / prefix are available to be checked out by members.

Size:
S Small books up to 8 inches in height. Generally mass-market paperbacks.
L Large books up to 11 inches in height. Generally mass-market hardcovers.
VL Very large books up to 12 inches in height. Often reference or art books.
XL eXtra large books bigger then 12 inches in height. Coffee table books.
F Foreign books. These can be S or L. Foreign books larger than that are kept with the English version
(none) A section of books that can be any size

Type:
(none) Fiction. This is the default value if only a size is present
A Anthologies
ART Art books
ARTIFACT Weird non-book stuff that doesn't really belong anywhere else
B Booklets. Small chapbooks, mostly dating from the 50s.
BLB Big Little Books. Very small (physically) books that were marketed to children.
CAL Calendars
CX Comics
DICT Dictionaries

SCRIPT Scripts

V Valuable. First editions, misprints, very old stuff.
